What Changed

  • Enterprise expansion accelerated: Three upsell deals totaling $98K closed this week versus a $60K forecast, driven by the new analytics add-on launched in Feb. The add-on attach rate is now 34% among Enterprise accounts.
  • SMB churn increased: Three SMB customers churned ($18K combined ARR), all citing budget reductions tied to Q1 cost-cutting. Win-back outreach is scheduled for next week.
  • Pipeline generation strong: Marketing sourced 42 new MQLs (+15% WoW) from the webinar series. Conversion to SQL running at 28%, above the 25% benchmark.

Risks & Opportunities

Risk Enterprise deal slippage

Two Enterprise deals ($220K and $160K) slipped from Q1 to Q2 due to customer procurement delays. Combined impact: $380K potential miss against Q1 target. Confidence: High.

Risk NPS decline

NPS dropped 2 points to 62, driven by support response time complaints. If unaddressed, churn could accelerate in Mid-Market segment. Confidence: Medium.

Opportunity Partner channel traction

Partner channel (launched Jan 15) generated its first two referral deals worth $45K. Early signal suggests 10-15% of Q2 pipeline could come from partners. Confidence: Medium.

Decisions Needed

  • Approve Q1 close plan: Should we offer a 10% discount incentive to pull the two slipped Enterprise deals ($380K) back into Q1? Recommendation: Yes, net margin impact is acceptable at $38K.
  • Support staffing: Approve hiring two additional support engineers to address NPS decline. Cost: $180K/yr. Recommendation: Approve — projected churn reduction worth $400K+ in saved ARR.
